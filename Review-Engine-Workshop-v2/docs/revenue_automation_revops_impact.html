Automation Only scenario: Focuses purely on cost reduction. 26.25 freed hours mean you need only 0.76 FTE for operations, so labor drops from $109.2K to $40.95K, saving $68.3K annually. This is achievable through headcount reduction or redeployment.

The net profit improvement is lower ($71.2K vs $68.3K) because you're not reducing labor costs‚Äîyou're converting a cost into revenue generation capacity.

üîç Detailed Financial Breakdown

Baseline (Current State): 42 hours/week of operations across 4 categories (Digital Marketing 9.25hrs, Social Media 11.25hrs, Loyalty Rewards 7.25hrs, Ecommerce 14.25hrs). Full 2.02 FTE team on payroll at $109.2K/year.
Automation Potential: 26.25 hours/week (62.5%) of tasks can be automated. Remaining 15.75 hours/week (37.5%) requires manual review/judgment. Automation reduces labor requirement from 2.02 FTE to 0.76 FTE.
Automation Only Scenario: If headcount reduced to 0.76 FTE, labor cost drops $68.3K annually. Revenue remains flat at $698K (no growth). Profit improves from $139.6K to $207.9K (+$68.3K, +49% improvement).
RevOps Reallocation (Internal): Keep 2.02 FTE on payroll. Reallocate freed hours (26.25/week) to: Customer Success & Retention (8 hrs), Product Development (6 hrs), Revenue Growth Initiatives (7 hrs), Process Improvement (5.25 hrs). Labor cost unchanged at $109.2K.
üí∞ Revenue Growth Model: 12% conservative annual growth rate applied to $698K baseline = $83.8K additional revenue. Assumes 7 hours/week dedicated to revenue growth initiatives (partnerships, pricing optimization, market expansion, upsell programs) generates this level of growth.
Operating Costs in RevOps Scenario: Base operating costs ($449.2K) remain stable. Incremental revenue of $83.8K requires ~15% additional operating costs for delivery/support = $12.6K additional. Total OpEx: $461.8K.
Net Profit for RevOps Scenario: $781.8K (revenue) - $109.2K (labor) - $461.8K (operating) = $210.8K. This is +$71.2K (+51%) better than baseline, but only +$3K better than automation-only scenario because labor wasn't reduced.
Key Difference: Automation-only saves money immediately (+$68.3K). RevOps-reallocation saves money from avoided headcount while generating revenue growth, resulting in similar profit but with growth trajectory and strategic positioning for future scale.
